The discovery of intelligent, non-carbon-based life on Xylos raised a host of complex ethical questions. How should humanity approach first contact? How could we ensure that our exploration did not harm this unique life form and its environment? Dr. Vance dedicated the rest of her life to answering these questions. She became a leading voice for a new, more cautious and respectful approach to space exploration. She argued that the prime directive of astrobiology should not be to exploit or even just to study, but to listen.
