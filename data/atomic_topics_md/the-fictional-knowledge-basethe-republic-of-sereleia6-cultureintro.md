# 6. Culture — Overview

Sereleian culture is a rich tapestry woven from its Celtic and Norse roots, its long history of isolation, and its deep respect for nature. **Language and Literature** The official language is Sereleian (Sereleisk), a language that evolved from a blend of Old Norse and Gaelic. It is known for its complex grammar and its rich vocabulary for describing natural phenomena. English is widely spoken as a second language. Sereleian literature has a strong oral tradition. Storytelling, or "Faeling," is a revered art form. The great epic of Sereleian literature is "The Song of the Sea Mothers," a collection of tales about the legendary Morrigna of the Clan Era, passed down orally for generations before being written down in the 20th century. **Music and Art** Music is central to Sereleian life. Traditional music is characterized by haunting melodies played on the "Vindra," a stringed instrument similar to a harp, and the "Holk," a deep-toned wooden flute. Sereleian art is dominated by intricate knotwork patterns, similar to Celtic art but with a unique maritime influence, incorporating motifs of waves, seabirds, and marine animals. This "Mare-Knot" style is found in everything from textile weaving to architecture. **Cuisine** Sereleian cuisine is based on fresh, locally sourced ingredients. Seafood is the staple of the diet, with dishes like "Kraken Stew" (a rich stew of various shellfish) and "Salt-Cured Selkie" (a type of dried and smoked fish) being national favorites. The islands are also home to a variety of unique plants, such as the "Sun-Berry," a tart, golden berry that is used to make jams and liquors. A significant cultural event is the "Long Night's Feast," a community meal held on the winter solstice.
